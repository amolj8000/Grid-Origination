All the Ways Gas Prices Benefit the Platform
1. Spark Spread (the core use)
Spark Spread = Power Price − (Gas Price × Heat Rate)
For ERCOT:

HB_HOUSTON spark spread = HB_HOUSTON DA − (Henry Hub × 8.5 MMBtu/MWh)
LZ_WEST spark spread = LZ_WEST DA − (Waha × 8.5 MMBtu/MWh)

When LZ_WEST DA is $22/MWh and Waha is -$5/MMBtu, the West Texas gas plant spark spread = $22 − (−$5 × 8.5) = $64.50/MWh. Gas plants near Waha are enormously profitable right now with essentially free fuel. This explains why gas isn't clearing the market even when wind is curtailed — it's competing at negative fuel cost.
2. Implied Heat Rate (market-implied gas plant valuation)
Implied Heat Rate = Power Price ÷ Gas Price
This is the break-even heat rate — if a plant's actual heat rate is lower than this number, it's in-the-money. It's effectively the market's valuation of gas optionality. When implied HR spikes (like during Winter Storm Uri), it shows the grid is fully stressed. Plotting implied heat rate over time is a cleaner signal than power price alone because it strips out the gas price component.
3. Waha-HH Basis as a Congestion Proxy
The Waha discount to Henry Hub is driven by Permian gas pipeline takeaway capacity — exactly the same bottleneck that causes West Texas electricity congestion. The two basis spreads (gas basis and power basis) are structurally correlated:

When Waha blows out negative → too much West Texas gas → gas plants run cheap → suppresses LZ_WEST power prices → worsens DA-RT spread for wind/solar
This correlation means Waha-HH spread is actually a leading indicator of ERCOT West Texas power congestion

Plotting Waha-HH basis alongside LZ_WEST DA-RT power basis on the same chart would be genuinely novel and analytically powerful for the platform.
4. Merit Order / Price Floor Modeling
Gas price sets the variable cost floor for ~70% of ERCOT's thermal fleet. At HH $3.50/MMBtu × 8.5 HR = $29.75/MWh breakeven. When hub prices drop below that, gas backs off and renewables dominate — but the constraint is transmission, not demand. This is the mechanism behind negative prices and curtailment. The platform can model: "at what gas price does curtailment start increasing?"
5. Heat Rate Options / Gas Plant Optionality
For trading: a gas peaker plant is essentially a call option on the spark spread. Its value = max(Power − Gas × HR, 0). When power spikes (ERCOT scarcity events), the plant captures enormous value. The app could calculate the implied value of ERCOT's peaker fleet as a function of current gas prices — showing when it's cheaper for Walmart to buy power vs hold a PPA.
6. PPA Pricing Anchor
Gas price is Walmart's outside option — if gas is cheap, wholesale power is cheap, and a PPA needs to be priced accordingly to be competitive. Plotting Henry Hub against ERCOT hub DA prices over time shows the long-run relationship: power tracks gas with a lag, and the gap widens when renewables displace gas. A buyer seeing widening PPA-vs-gas discount knows the PPA hedge is becoming more valuable.
7. Negative Price Prediction Signal
Negative Waha gas prices → gas generators bid near zero or negative (fuel credit) → prices spike negative when wind ramps → curtailment increases. Current Waha negativity at -$5.69 is directly causing West Texas negative power price frequency to remain high, which is exactly what your curtailment scores should be capturing.
8. Portfolio Hedging / Options Pricing
If you're going further into the trading angle: gas-power correlation (correlation between HH and HB_NORTH) can be calculated from the historical data. Low correlation months are when renewable displacing is happening; high correlation months are gas-dominated. This ratio matters for hedge effectiveness calculations in the Basis Analyzer.

EIA API Implementation Notes
The easiest path for the app is to call two free endpoints:
# Henry Hub monthly (no auth)
https://api.eia.gov/v2/natural-gas/pri/fut/data/?
  frequency=monthly&data[0]=value&facets[series][]=RNGWHHDM&api_key=YOUR_KEY

# Henry Hub daily via FRED (truly no auth needed)
https://fred.stlouisfed.org/graph/fredgraph.csv?id=DHHNGSP

# Waha monthly (EIA regional spot prices)
https://api.eia.gov/v2/natural-gas/pri/sum/data/?
  frequency=monthly&data[0]=value&facets[duoarea][]=Y35NY&api_key=YOUR_KEY
EIA API keys are free and instant at eia.gov/opendata. FRED requires no key at all for CSV downloads. This keeps the implementation fully free and public — no subscription data needed.

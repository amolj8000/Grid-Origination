PyPSA is not mainly a forecasting library. It is an open-source Python framework for optimizing and simulating modern power and energy systems, including generators, renewables, storage, transmission constraints, unit commitment, sector coupling, and linearized power flow.

What PyPSA can be used for
1. Economic dispatch simulation

This is probably the most useful next feature for your app.

You can use PyPSA to answer:

Given load, generation availability, marginal costs, and network constraints, which generators should dispatch and what prices result?

For your ERCOT app, this could become:

“ERCOT Dispatch Simulator”

Users could change assumptions like:

Wind output
Solar output
Gas price / generator marginal cost
Load growth
Outage/derate assumptions
Transmission limits

Then the app shows:

Dispatch by generator type
Simulated marginal prices
Price separation by zone/node
Congestion impact
Curtailment

PyPSA supports economic dispatch and market-style optimization problems.

2. Linear optimal power flow / LMP simulation

This is the direct extension of your congestion work.

PyPSA can simulate power flows across a network and calculate marginal prices at buses under network constraints. Its examples cover electricity markets, linear optimal power flow, grid modelling, and congestion-type cases.

For your app, this could become:

“Why Did This Node Separate?”

User selects a node, zone, or hub, and the app shows:

Which simplified constraint is binding
How much flow is moving across paths
Which region has cheap generation trapped
Why node-to-hub basis widened
Simulated before/after LMPs

This would be very compelling because your existing congestion model says where risk exists, while PyPSA can help explain why the risk exists.

3. Renewable curtailment analysis

PyPSA is useful for modelling variable wind and solar generation.

For ERCOT, this is a strong feature because congestion, negative pricing, and curtailment often connect to renewable-heavy regions.

Possible app feature:

“Renewable Curtailment & Negative Price Simulator”

Scenarios:

West Texas wind increases 20%
Solar output increases during low-load hours
Export capacity from West is reduced
Load does not absorb renewable production
Storage is added at a constrained node

Outputs:

Renewable curtailment
Negative price risk
Local LMP suppression
Congestion rent
Basis impact

This would be very relevant for renewable developers, traders, and risk teams.

4. Storage optimization

PyPSA can model inter-temporal storage operation, including charging/discharging across time periods.

For your app:

“Battery Revenue & Congestion Relief Simulator”

Questions it can answer:

Where would a battery be most valuable?
Does storage reduce local negative pricing?
Does storage reduce congestion?
What is the arbitrage value between low-price and high-price intervals?
How does battery dispatch change during congestion events?

Outputs:

Charge/discharge schedule
Simulated revenue
LMP impact
Congestion reduction
Curtailment reduction

This is one of the best portfolio features because it connects ERCOT congestion, renewables, storage, and market value.

5. Transmission expansion / constraint relief

PyPSA can be used for capacity expansion planning, including trade-offs between generation, storage, and transmission infrastructure.

For your app:

“Transmission Constraint Relief Simulator”

Scenarios:

Increase transfer capacity from West to North
Add a new simplified link between zones
Increase line rating by 10%, 20%, 50%
Compare congestion before and after upgrade

Outputs:

Reduction in congestion hours
Reduction in average basis
Reduction in negative pricing
Change in dispatch
Change in congestion rent
Economic value of added transfer capacity

This would be very impressive because it moves from market analytics into planning-style analysis.

6. Capacity expansion planning

PyPSA can optimize investments in generation, storage, and transmission over time.

For your app, this could become a later-stage feature:

“Where Should New Capacity Be Built?”

The model could test:

New solar in West
New battery in Houston
New gas peaker in North
New transmission between West and North
New load growth from data centers

Outputs:

Optimal added capacity
Expected dispatch
Congestion impact
Simulated price impact
Curtailment impact
System cost impact

This is probably not MVP, but it is a strong Phase 2 or Phase 3 feature.

7. Unit commitment

PyPSA can model unit commitment for conventional generators, including on/off commitment decisions.

For your app:

“Generator Commitment Simulator”

It could help show:

Which thermal units would be online/offline
How minimum generation affects prices
How ramping or commitment constraints affect scarcity
How thermal fleet flexibility impacts congestion and price spikes

This is more complex and may require better generator-level data, so I would treat it as advanced.

8. Scarcity / load shedding simulation

PyPSA examples include load shedding and value-of-lost-load style modelling in capacity expansion scenarios.

For ERCOT:

“Scarcity Event Simulator”

Scenarios:

High load
Low wind
Reduced thermal availability
Transmission derate
Load growth from data centers
Limited import/export capacity between regions

Outputs:

Unserved energy, if any
Scarcity price proxy
Reserve-tightness signal
Price spike risk
Most vulnerable zones

This could complement your forecasting layer nicely.

9. Multi-period scenario analysis

PyPSA is designed to scale across large networks and long time series.

For your app, this means you can run scenarios across:

One interval
One day
One week
One representative month
Seasonal cases
High-wind / low-load days
Peak summer days
Winter storm-like stress cases

This matters because congestion is not just a single-interval problem. It has time-dependent behavior.
